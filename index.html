Spatiotemporal Detection of Terahertz Electric Fields

A concise guide to measuring the temporal waveform, spatial beam profile, spectral content, and polarization state of a broadband THz pulse.

Start readingRelated APL publication

What does “spatiotemporal detection” mean?

A conventional THz time-domain measurement records the electric field as a function of delay at one position. Spatiotemporal detection repeats the measurement across the transverse plane, producing a data set with two spatial coordinates and one time coordinate.

E(x, y, t)   →   E(x, y, f)
1

Temporal information

The waveform shows arrival time, pulse shape, oscillations, and temporal delay.

2

Spatial information

Each pixel or scan position provides the local THz field across the beam profile.

3

Polarization information

Two orthogonal field components allow the local polarization state to be reconstructed.

Measurement workflow

The experiment combines time-domain sampling with spatially resolved detection. The exact optical arrangement depends on the detector, but the processing sequence is broadly similar.

Acquire a THz waveform at every spatial position

Scan the optical delay while recording the detector response for each pixel or transverse coordinate.

Measure orthogonal field components

Record two independent projections, represented as Ex(x,y,t) and Ey(x,y,t).

Remove offsets and define the time window

Subtract the temporal background, suppress unreliable pixels, and retain the region containing the THz transient.

Transform from time to frequency

Apply a Fourier transform along the time axis to obtain complex spectral amplitude and phase at every spatial position.

Calculate field and polarization maps

Use the complex orthogonal components to form intensity, phase, polarization-angle, ellipticity, and Stokes-parameter maps.

Visualize the complete field

Display time-resolved movies, frequency-resolved maps, vector fields, and polarization distributions.

Time-domain field

  • Shows the local THz electric-field waveform.
  • Reveals pulse arrival time and propagation delay.
  • Allows movies of E(x,y,t) to be generated frame by frame.

Frequency-domain field

  • Shows complex amplitude and phase at a selected frequency.
  • Allows spatial beam evolution to be compared across the THz spectrum.
  • Separates frequency-dependent structure hidden in the broadband pulse.

Spatially resolved polarization

From Ex and Ey, the Stokes parameters are:

S0 = |Ex|² + |Ey|²
S1 = |Ex|² − |Ey|²
S2 = 2 Re(ExEy*)
S3 = 2 Im(ExEy*)
Important: reliable polarization maps require adequate signal-to-noise ratio in both field components. Mask low-intensity pixels before interpreting normalized Stokes parameters.
